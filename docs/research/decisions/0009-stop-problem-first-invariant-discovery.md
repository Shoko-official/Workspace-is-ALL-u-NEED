## Findings

### Six incidents fail two-factor causal necessity

The revocation race, forked-effect duplication, purpose violation,
declared-state incompleteness, and repeated cost trigger all occur in ordinary
deterministic state machines; adaptive computation is not necessary. Real-time
snapshot staleness occurs with transient sensor state; governed persistent state
is not necessary. These failures are structural, so an LLM experiment cannot
repair them.

### The two remaining incidents are established generic objects

Deletion after a record has entered parameters, fast weights, caches, or
summaries is machine unlearning with dependency closure and a canonical-
retraining comparator. A recent behavioral-audit result establishes a privacy
trade-off only for its declared convex-model setting; M1g derives neither a
universal audit impossibility nor a stronger verifier.

History-dependent compute disparity can require both persistent history and an
adaptive allocator, but around any fixed point `d*=F(d*)` its proposed
multiplier is `F'(d*)` and its local exponent is `log|F'(d*)|`. That is the
ordinary Jacobian/Lyapunov stability test inside a fair sequential-resource-
control problem.

### Exact formulas do not leave a distinct residual

The strongest quantitative escape was the real-time objective
`J(b)=q(b)S(tau(b))-c(b)`. Its derivative precisely says when marginal anytime
quality is outweighed by environment-state hazard and cost. Real-Time Reasoning
Agents directly occupies the LLM incident, while success-probability control of
deliberation dates to Yamada's 1996 planning work. The formula is an engineering
decision rule obtained by the product and chain rules, not a new state-compute
law.

The denial-of-wallet bound is similarly exact but inherited. Under paired
counterfactual requests and randomness, if a retained write has conditional
activation probability at least `p` per round and conditional marginal cost at
least `kappa` on activation, expected descendant cost grows at least as
`H p kappa`. Principal-level lifetime quotas bound it by construction. AgentDoS
and Resource Containers already occupy the application and generic accounting
branches.

### Complete no-recycling audit

The M1g register crosswalks all 73 prior formulations. It distinguishes
`RECYCLED`, `REDUCED`, and `NO_REOPENING`; the last means only that no M1g result
reopens the exact earlier class. The closest surface residual, unique descent
from clonable continuations, reduces to state continuity, fork-linearizability,
at-most-once effects, communication, credentials, or an external non-clonable
resource.

## Decision

Accept global `STOP` for all eight M1g problem-first attempts.

- No object is admitted.
- No object is `DISTINCT_CANDIDATE`.
- No theorem, estimand, invariant, bound, capability, or transportable law
  remains specific to governed persistent LLM state plus adaptive computation.
- No model, benchmark, implementation, training run, experiment, paid compute,
  proof project, manuscript, or submission is authorized.
- The direction-setting objective remains active, but this cycle does not
  supply its article.

Independent scientific, bibliographic, and governance reviews all passed on
the same fixed scientific commit. Acceptance does not waive protected CI or
issue-closing integration.

## Anti-rewrite determination

The following are explicitly insufficient for a future article:

- presenting operational incidents already studied by systems, security,
  unlearning, fairness, or real-time planning as a new LLM theory;
- publishing the P05 product-rule derivative or P08 linear-expectation bound as
  a novel law without a non-inherited residual;
- renaming linearizability, fork consistency, at-most-once effects,
  non-interference, declassification, machine unlearning, fair MDPs,
  verification, resource principals, quotas, or leases;
- weakening the comparator by withholding epochs, cancellation, replay,
  complete state, communication, external effect IDs, policy labels, or
  principal accounting;
- treating a useful engineering checklist or the eight negative incidents as a
  survey, taxonomy, manifesto, roadmap, maturity model, or direction-setting
  article; or
- running a benchmark to manufacture novelty after the object gate has failed.

## Alternatives considered

- `GO` on in-flight revocation was rejected because adaptive compute is not
  necessary and linearizability plus epoch validation closes the effect race.
- `GO` on behavioral deletion was rejected by machine unlearning and the lack
  of a new exact audit result.
- `GO` on forked continuation identity was rejected because clonable isolated
  state cannot provide unique descent without an established external anchor.
- `GO` on purpose-scoped influence was rejected by direct persistent-memory
  incidents and ordinary IFC/non-interference.
- `GO` on real-time snapshot survival was rejected by the two-factor gate,
  direct current work, and the older persistence-probability stopping rule.
- `GO` on service disparity was rejected by fair sequential MDPs and generic
  dynamical stability.
- `GO` on declared-state completeness was rejected as an observational-
  determinism hyperproperty and generic verification problem.
- `GO` on denial of wallet was rejected by direct AgentDoS evidence and
  principal-level amortized resource accounting.
- A minimum experiment was rejected because none of these failures is an
  empirical-identification gap.
