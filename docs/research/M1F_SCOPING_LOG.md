## Exact primary-record resolutions

### Upstream report and mission tokenization

The report's central sections 6 and 8.2 combine four different objects:

1. domain vocabulary extension;
2. recommender-system item tokenization;
3. model or serving routing; and
4. adaptive inference and heterogeneous hardware.

The primary records do not establish that composition.

- Herold et al. (`EV-0166`) extend an existing tokenizer with domain tokens and
  guarantee no increase in token count. Their reported e-commerce reduction is
  up to 20%; they do not define a mission router or a model swarm.
- UniTok (`EV-0167`) maps semantic embeddings of recommendation items to
  discrete item identifiers using an autoencoder, codebooks, and TokenMoE. It
  does not segment text, code, or mathematical expressions. The report's
  "expert tokenizer Code" interpretation is unsupported.
- Dynamic tokenization (`EV-0168`) and Ted-Tok (`EV-0169`) already adapt
  segmentation or vocabulary to inputs and temporal drift. They occupy the
  genuine lexical-adaptation branch.
- Rerouting LLM Routers is an adversarial-integrity study, not a cost-free
  mission orchestrator. Dual-Pool routes by an estimated token budget and
  explicitly avoids running a tokenizer in the router.
- The report's component-level gains come from different tasks, baselines,
  models, and accounting regimes. They cannot be multiplied or promoted to a
  system-level energy claim.

The exact reversible-codec construction in M1F-O08 closes the apparent
writer-reader novelty. Non-injective item or latent codebooks instead introduce
ordinary information loss.

### Shared controller and correlated sampling

For any randomized joint circuit `J(h,r)=(m,c)` with common input and seed, two
parallel copies reproduce the exact joint law with at most twice the work,
twice the aggregate fast memory, and the same depth. Sequential copies preserve
the original fast-memory ceiling with at most twice the depth. For a finite
output alphabet, a single copy plus transmission of one output gives the
alternative communication bound. Private inputs, isolated memories, or private
randomness move the object into communication, pebbling, or channel synthesis.

SFRL (`EV-0160`) and multiprocessor pebbling (`EV-0161`) delimit these generic
branches. TriRoute (`EV-0162`) independently supplies the recent empirical
shared-trunk architecture over attention, experts/depth, and cache precision.

### Authority, privacy, and executable state

- TMA-NM (`EV-0158`) states the origin-binding authority object and its
  laundering channels directly. f-secure and CaMeL (`EV-0156`; `EV-0157`)
  provide information-flow and capability defenses.
- Ratliff and Vadhan (`EV-0159`) define privacy when both output and runtime are
  observable. Treating one full interaction as a randomized program and its
  per-step depth, stopping-time, and latency vector as output therefore does not
  define a new privacy primitive. No adaptive-composition theorem or broader
  address, branch, call, or action trace is attributed to this source.
- A universal sound and complete verifier for arbitrary executable writes is a
  program-verification impossibility. SEVerA (`EV-0163`) supplies the restricted
  contract-and-fallback construction rather than a universal gate.

### Strategic state contribution

Online information-acquisition mechanisms (`EV-0164`) already optimize receiver
utility while enforcing truthful sender reports under full or bandit feedback.
Adaptive costly-audit mechanisms (`EV-0165`) already state regret and audit
tradeoffs for strategic repeated allocation. Persisting a report does not alter
their private-information or incentive classes.

## Resolution notes

1. `Outside the 64` and `distinct` are separate decisions. Timing privacy and
   origin-bound authority were not earlier candidate labels, but one reduces to
   a generic output-channel class and the other is directly published.
2. A factor-two constant saving from shared common subexpressions is not an
   asymptotic state-compute separation. Forbidding a shared frontend only in the
   comparator manufactures the result.
3. A tokenizer changes sequence length and cost, but a reversible tokenizer
   does not change the underlying text information. Model-specific embeddings
   and caches are migration objects already screened in M1c.
4. Search silence is not used. Every disposition rests on a constructive
   compiler, exact transcode, direct positive record, generic reduction, or
   explicit recycling path.
5. Several 2026 sources are recent preprints. Their claims are used only to the
   scope justified by the inspected version and are never treated as replicated
   empirical truth.
